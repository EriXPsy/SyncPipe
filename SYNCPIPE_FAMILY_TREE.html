How two intellectual lineages — a theoretical one (what to measure) and a methodological one (how to test it) — shaped the architecture, the descriptor set, and the validation chain.

How to read this. The left column is the theoretical lineage (coordination dynamics → interpersonal-synchrony psychology → an episode/state view of coupling). It answers what to measure and drives the descriptor definitions (mean, peak, dwell, switching, bimodality, entropy, timing, occupancy). The right column is the methodological lineage (surrogate testing → block bootstrap → design controls → ground-truth simulation + incremental AUC). It answers how to test it and drives the null models, the GT batteries, and the AUC evidence. Both feed the five-layer architecture, whose spine is the three-step audited evidence chain. The greyed boxes mark the one capability honestly deferred to v2: a validated peak-timing existence null.
